About Independence

Indian Independence, achieved on August 15, 1947, is a landmark in the country's history, symbolizing the end of British colonial rule that lasted for nearly two centuries. The journey to independence was marked by a relentless struggle, uniting people across the nation in a common cause for freedom. This struggle was characterized by numerous movements and revolts, with leaders like Mahatma Gandhi advocating for non-violence and civil disobedience, and others like Subhas Chandra Bose taking a more militant approach. The independence movement gained momentum in the early 20th century, with significant events such as the Jallianwala Bagh Massacre in 1919, which fueled widespread anger against British rule, and the Salt March in 1930, a non-violent protest against British salt taxes. The Quit India Movement in 1942 further intensified the demand for British withdrawal from India.

Freedom Fighters

  • Mahatma Gandhi

    Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi ( 2 October 1869 - 30 January 1948) was an Indian lawyer, anti-colonial nationalist, and political ethicist who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule. He inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world

  • Bhagat Singh

    Bhagat Singh (27 September 1907 - 23 March 1931) was an Indian anti-colonial revolutionary, who participated in the mistaken murder of a junior British police officer in December 1928 in what was to be retaliation for the death of an Indian nationalist.

Independence Day Celebrations

India's Independence Day on August 15 commemorated its liberation from British rule in 1947. The day is marked by festivities, flag-raising ceremonies, and patriotic performances. Prime Minister's address at the Red Fort and cultural events in schools and colleges highlight the celebrations.

Flag Hoisting

Usually, the flag was prepared with flowers inside and tied to the post. A picture of a freedom fighter used to be placed below the flag. The principal of the school would do the flag hoisting followed by singing the national anthem

Parades

Parades and pageants showcase scenes from the independence struggle and India's diverse cultural traditions. Similar events take place in state capitals where the Chief Ministers of individual states unfurl the national flag, followed by parades and pageants

Cultural Programs

On Independence Day, people across India take part in various celebrations. The day begins with flag-hoisting ceremonies, where the national flag is proudly displayed. There are also parades and cultural events that showcase India's rich heritage and unity. Independence Day is a time for both pride and reflection
